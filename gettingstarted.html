Welcome

Hello and welcome to the exciting world of electronics!

In the Creative Electronics Guide you will learn how to make a connection between Scratch and Arduino to create interactive electronics projects.

The guide assumes that you have basic knowledge of Scratch. If you have never used Scratch before the Tips bar on the right hand side of the Scratch editor is a great place to start! This guide does not assume any knowledge of Arduino or electronics.

Things you need

To complete all of the projects in this guide you will need the following items:
  • Arduino/Genuino 101 board
  • USB Cable - Standard A-B.
  • White LEDs - They come in 3mm, 5mm, 10mm sizes. If you can't get white, any color will do.
  • 220 ohm resistors
  • 10K ohm resistors
  • Photo cell - We will use this as our light sensor.
  • Servo motor
  • Jumper wires
  • Breadboard
  • A computer with internet connection
  • Various arts and crafts supplies
  • Fiberfill (poly stuffing) or cotton balls

Getting Started

This guide will be organized into individual projects which you can navigate to by scrolling down the page.

The Preparation section will go over preparing your Arduino board to use with Scratch programming.
